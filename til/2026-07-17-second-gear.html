Critical / Reuters Xi pitches China as leader of new global AI order, challenging US dominance

Pre-read: AI governance is becoming a diplomatic architecture problem as much as a model-capability race. U.S. export controls and Western safety institutions give Washington leverage over chips and standards, while China's strongest counter-position is open-source access, Global South capacity building, and multilateral rulemaking. This Reuters piece is worth opening because it shows Beijing turning that frame into a formal geopolitical offer.

Summary: Reuters reports that Xi Jinping used the World Artificial Intelligence Conference in Shanghai to cast China as the champion of a new global AI order built around access, open-source technology, and developing-country participation. Xi warned against unequal access creating new historical injustices, urged countries to seize the open-source opportunity, and challenged U.S. influence over the rules of the sector. The report says China also pushed for a World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization, with analysts reading the move as an attempt to avoid becoming a rule-taker in a sector shaped by U.S. firms, export controls, and Washington-led partnerships. AP's parallel report adds that Xi criticized technology restrictions framed as national security and tied the pitch to training, meteorological AI support, and cooperation with blocs such as ASEAN, the African Union, and BRICS. The strategic tension is direct: China is offering AI access as soft power while still managing its own national-security constraints around advanced models.

Happenings GPT-5.6 shifts OpenAI toward agentic work

Pre-read: The frontier model contest is moving from chat quality toward long-horizon tool use, coding reliability, and cost per completed outcome. OpenAI is now packaging that shift into product surfaces, pricing tiers, and developer workflows rather than a single monolithic model release.

Summary: OpenAI Dev News framed GPT-5.6 as a three-model family for production workflows: Sol for the hardest agentic and coding tasks, Terra for everyday production work, and Luna for fast high-volume jobs. The newsletter says Sol is available in the API and Codex, and points developers toward a model guide, migration quickstart, and token-usage practices such as prompt caching and preserved reasoning context. OpenAI's public release page says Sol posts stronger long-horizon browsing, tool-use, computer-use, and coding results, while Terra and Luna are meant to make capability cheaper to deploy. The important product signal is ChatGPT Work plus Codex: OpenAI is steering builders toward agents that operate across apps, files, browser sessions, and repos, with model choice becoming an operational knob rather than a brand decision.
